# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Hibernia Economy to be a welcoming, harassment-free project for everyone
who takes part — contributors, maintainers, server staff, and players — regardless
of background or identity. We pledge to interact in ways that keep the community
open, respectful, and constructive.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and considerate; assume good faith.
- Welcome newcomers and differing viewpoints; give and accept constructive
  feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and the wider community.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologise, and learn from them.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Sexualised language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Personal or political attacks, insults, or demeaning comments.
- Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussion, code review, or the community.
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Project governance and final say

Hibernia Economy is a single-maintainer project. **Rían Errity** is the project
owner and lead maintainer, and **retains final decision-making authority over
everything that goes into it** — which features, changes, and pull requests are
accepted or rejected, the project's scope and design direction, and how the
components fit together.

Contributions are genuinely welcome, but a few things follow from this model:

- **Opening an issue or a pull request does not guarantee it will be merged.**
  Work may be declined — including complete, well-written, passing work — if it
  does not fit the project's direction.
- **Contributors do not unilaterally decide what belongs in the plugins.** Whether
  a new feature, behaviour, or change is in scope is the maintainer's call — not
  something a contributor gets to settle by opening a PR, merging it, or insisting
  on it.
- **Proposals and disagreement are welcome; final calls are not up for
  litigation.** Make the case for an idea on Discord — robust technical debate is
  encouraged — but once a decision has been made, please respect it.

This is not about shutting down discussion. It exists because the project runs a
**live, shared server economy**, where a single inconsistent change can affect
every player's money — so scope and coherence have to be held by someone
accountable for the whole. To propose or discuss work, start on the
**[Hibernia Economy Discord](https://discord.gg/b88K3ETeHS)** (see
[CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)).

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the repository, pull
requests, code review, the Discord, and anywhere else connected to the project —
and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting and enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, report it privately to a
maintainer on the **[Hibernia Economy Discord](https://discord.gg/b88K3ETeHS)** —
ask to open a private report and we will move it somewhere confidential. (For
*security* vulnerabilities, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.)

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and may
take any action they consider appropriate — including editing or removing
contributions, and warning, temporarily restricting, or permanently banning
anyone — for behaviour they consider inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.
Reports will be handled with discretion, respecting the privacy and safety of
whoever reports.
