# Government salaries

The government pays a regular **salary** to players who hold official roles — senators,
secretaries, justices, officers, and so on. It's fully automatic: if your role has a
salary, you're paid on a timer while you're online and active. No command needed.

## How you get paid

- Salaries are paid **while you're online**, on a repeating timer (about every **15
  minutes** by default).
- The amount is tied to your **role**. If you hold more than one salaried role, you're
  paid the **highest single one** — salaries don't stack.
- The money comes from a **government account**, paid straight into your balance.

When a salary lands, you'll see a message in chat:

> You received **65.00** in government salary.

> [!NOTE]
> You're only paid while you're **online**. If you're offline when the timer fires, you
> don't get that round — and roles that don't have a salary set simply pay nothing.

> [!NOTE]
> Being **AFK** counts as not active: by default, online-but-AFK players are skipped for
> that round. (Staff can turn this off, but it's on by default.)

## Checking your pay

Salary payments show up in your history like any other income:

```text
/transactions
```

You can also see them on your **My data** dashboard on the
[Explorer](/docs/guides/using-the-explorer) — they appear as income from the
government.

> [!NOTE]
> Salary amounts are set by server staff and tied to your in-game role — there's no
> command to change your own. If you think your salary is wrong, raise it with staff.
